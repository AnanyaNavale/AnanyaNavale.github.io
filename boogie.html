UX Design · Accessibility

Boogie

A ride-share experience designed for everyone – especially those who maps leave behind.

Timeline Winter 2025, 6 weeks
Role Lead Front-End Designer, Researcher
Tools Figma, React Native, Expo
Class CS 377Q: Designing for Accessibility

Boogie is a ride-share prototype designed with blind and low-vision (BLV) users at the center – though built to be usable by anyone. I led front-end design and also conducted user interviews, usability tests, and data collection across the project's six weeks.

The design challenge I'm most proud of solving is map-free navigation. Most ride-share apps default to visual maps for selecting pickup and drop-off points – an experience that excludes BLV users entirely. Boogie replaces that with a screen-reader-friendly, landmark-based location flow that doesn't require sight to use confidently.

I also incorporated imagery and a color mode toggle for low-vision users who still rely on their own vision to navigate interfaces and may be sensitive to light. The use of images was also a universal design choice – since Boogie is for everyone, visual cues benefit sighted users too, without compromising accessibility for anyone.
