LunaCart

Healthy late-night eating, decided before the cravings hit.

Timeline Winter 2026, 10 weeks
Role Lead Developer, Project Manager, Researcher
Tools React Native, Express, PostgreSQL, Drizzle ORM, Figma
Class CS 247B: Design for Behavior Change

LunaCart is a mobile app that helps Stanford students eat healthier late at night – not by restricting what they eat, but by shifting when they decide. Students pre-order healthy snacks in the morning, before fatigue and cravings take over, making an intentional commitment while their cognitive resources are still intact.

I led development of almost the entirety of the prototype, ran our observational and intervention studies, managed most deliverables, and served as de facto project manager across the team. The piece I'm most proud of is the behavior-logging mechanism: after following through on their order, users answer a few simple, emotion-based questions – no calorie counts, no restrictions – and get rewarded with a randomly generated song added to their Growth Playlist. It's low-pressure, personal, and a little bit surprising every time.
