Frontseat Baby

Drive your listening experiences.

Timeline Winter 2026, 10 weeks
Role Prototype Developer, UX Researcher
Tools Figma, React Native, Expo, EAS
Class CS 206: Exploring Computational Journalism

Frontseat Baby is a prototype exploring two features KQED was looking to bring to their app: a skippable livestream presented as a playlist, and a chatbot-driven interface for generating personalized story queues. The goal was to give listeners more agency over how they experience public radio.

Within the team, I took on the prototype end-to-end – designing and building the front-end from scratch, deploying it via EAS build, and leading the user study from survey design through testing. It was a heavy lift toward the end of the quarter, but seeing it hold up as a real, testable platform made it worth it.
